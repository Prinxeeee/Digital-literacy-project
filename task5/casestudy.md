## Case Study (Phishing Attack)

Phishing is a type of cybercrime in which attackers impersonate legitimate organizations to trick individuals into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, or OTPs. A common example involves emails or messages that appear to be from trusted sources like banks, e-commerce platforms, or educational institutions.

In a typical phishing scenario, the attacker first creates a fake message that looks authentic, often using official logos and urgent language. The message usually contains a link directing the victim to a fraudulent website that closely resembles a real one. When the victim clicks the link and enters their login credentials or financial details, the information is immediately captured by the attacker. In some cases, victims may also receive calls posing as bank officials requesting OTPs.

College students are frequent targets because they actively use online platforms for payments, social media, and academic work, sometimes without strong security awareness. The consequences of phishing can be severe, including unauthorized bank transactions, identity theft, and loss of personal data. Victims may also experience emotional stress and financial instability.

This case highlights the importance of verifying sources, avoiding suspicious links, and never sharing confidential information online. Awareness and caution are key to preventing such cybercrimes.
